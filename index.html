    2. Foundation

      3. The earliest known definite reference to cricket is to it being played in South East England in the mid-16th century. It spread globally with the expansion of the British Empire, with the first international matches in the second half of the 19th century.

    3. Expansions and termination

      4. In cricket, "expansion" generally refers to increasing the number of teams participating in a league or tournament, while "termination" refers to the removal of a team from a competition. These changes can be driven by various factors like commercial interests, player availability, and strategic decisions.

  2. Administration

    3. The ICC, in the context of cricket, stands for the International Cricket Council. It is the global governing body for the sport, responsible for organizing and governing major international cricket tournaments like the Cricket World Cup, setting the rules of the game, and appointing match officials. Essentially, the ICC oversees all aspects of international cricket.
